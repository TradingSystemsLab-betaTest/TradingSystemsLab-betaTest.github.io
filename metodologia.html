Strategy Development and Selection Methodology

Fundamentals

Our approach to systematic trading is based on the conviction that a strategy's robustness stems from a structured, objective, and repeatable process. The methodology is not just a series of technical steps, but a framework that integrates market characteristic-driven development, conscious risk management, and strategic development oriented toward the operator's psychological sustainability.

Development Process

Below is a summary of our workflow, which starts from the initial idea and proceeds through to the complete validation of the strategy.

1. Study of Market Characteristics

The first essential phase is studying the movements of the market we intend to operate on. Questions to ask concern, for example, trading volumes, conditions of higher volatility, analysis of underlying trends, the type of operators present, and the most suitable time horizon. In-depth study at this stage allows us to develop strategies in line with the intrinsic characteristics of the market.

2. Development of Trading Logic

Once the nature of the instrument is understood, we define entry conditions by evaluating the most suitable and profitable approach. Subsequent steps include risk management and the integration of any operational filters, maintaining focus on robustness to avoid the risk of overfitting. It is essential to follow a data analysis-driven development, aligning with the actual tendencies of the instrument.

3. Backtesting and Validation

Despite efforts to avoid overfitting, a strategy's robustness must always be demonstrated through rigorous verification levels. Our protocol includes In-Sample Testing, Walk-Forward Analysis and rigorous Out-of-Sample validation, followed by a period of demo trading or minimum size trading. This process allows us to discard models that don't keep their statistical promises, selecting only the most stable and performing strategies for the live environment.

Portfolio Composition

Finally, in the portfolio composition phase, selection criteria encompass various parameters that are evaluated cyclically. From diversification, aimed at avoiding excessive correlation, to operational efficiency: the goal is to evaluate the real added value of each individual system and its contribution to the overall stability of the portfolio.

Evaluation Metrics

Below are some of the most important metrics that guide the development and validation of our trading strategies.

Metric Description Target Value / Interpretation
Net Profit Net profit after commission costs. Must be significant relative to the risk taken.
Max Drawdown (MDD) Maximum loss recorded from a previous peak. Must align with the operator's psychological tolerance.
Sharpe Ratio Risk-adjusted return (volatility). >1.0 (ideal >1.5) indicates good compensation for the risk taken.
Profit Factor Ratio of total profits to total losses. A value above 1.5 suggests good logic efficiency.
Win Rate Percentage of trades closed in profit. Not primary. A moderate WR with a high average profit is often preferable.
Expectancy Average expected profit per single trade. Must be positive, stable, and substantial relative to operational costs.

Conclusion: A Dynamic and Adaptive Process

The development and management of systematic strategies represent a continuous cycle of learning and adaptation. Real trading results and market evolution can lead to refining logics, updating parameters, or temporarily suspending a strategy from the active portfolio.

The ultimate goal is not the search for the perfect strategy, but the construction of robust and diversified systems that, collectively, offer a positive statistical expectation and sustainable operation in the long term. The methodology described here is the framework that makes this goal achievable and measurable.
