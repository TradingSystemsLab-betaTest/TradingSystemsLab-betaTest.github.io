Trend Following

8 February 2026

Trend following is one of the most widely used systematic trading methodologies in financial markets. The basic idea is simple: identify an existing trend and follow it until its conclusion, maximizing profits from the market’s directional movements. However, this conceptual simplicity hides several operational complexities that require clear rules, rigorous risk management, and discipline.

Mean Reversion

8 February 2026

Mean reversion is a systematic trading approach based on the assumption that prices, after deviating significantly from a reference mean, tend to revert to it over time. Unlike trend-following strategies, mean reversion aims to exploit temporary deviations caused by market excesses or short-term inefficiencies.

Breakout Strategies

8 February 2026

MBreakout strategies are a systematic trading approach designed to exploit the onset of significant directional price movements when the market breaks through key support or resistance levels. Unlike mean reversion strategies, which focus on price returning to equilibrium, breakout systems assume that a meaningful price break can trigger volatility expansion and sustained directional moves.

Market Bias and Seasonal Strategies

8 February 2026

Bias and seasonal strategies exploit recurring patterns and predictable market behaviors tied to psychological factors, economic cycles, or specific times of the year. These approaches are not based on traditional technical or fundamental analysis, but on historical statistics and temporal recurrences.
