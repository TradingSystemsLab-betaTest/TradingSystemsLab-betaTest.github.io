Trading System Development: Analysis of Approaches

The development of trading systems is primarily based on these approaches: Trend Following, Breakout, Mean Reverting, Bias, or specific technical analysis patterns.

The choice of the optimal approach is dictated by the characteristics of the analyzed market or the chosen time frame; each instrument possesses its own "character" that determines its movements.

Trend Following

This approach finds its maximum effectiveness in instruments driven by strong underlying directionality. It aims to exploit the inertia of a market, exiting positions only when a trend reversal is underway.

Mean Reverting

This is the ideal approach for markets showing a marked tendency to retrace after breaking key levels. This behavior is typical of Forex pairs or highly liquid instruments, where the "heaviness" of the order book prevents breakouts from expanding into impulsive movements, bringing the price back towards the mean.

Breakout

It is based on exploiting the volatility explosion when a static level is broken, such as the previous day's highs. Instruments like cryptocurrencies lend themselves to this approach, but often risk suffering from high slippage.

Bias

This approach focuses on identifying repetitive movements and cyclical inefficiencies, not only intraday but also at weekly or monthly levels. It is particularly effective in Forex, where it's possible to discover recurring patterns linked to specific times or market sessions.

Technical Patterns

Finally, technical patterns make sense on higher timeframes, where we can identify recurring patterns for both reversal and trend following. Mature markets can provide many satisfactions with this approach, provided one is willing to accept slower trading with sometimes prolonged drawdowns.
