Creating a Trend Following Strategy

Published on: February 8, 2026

Trend following is one of the most well-established systematic trading methodologies. The core idea is simple: identify an existing trend and follow it until it ends, maximizing profits from directional market movements.

What Is Trend Following

A trend-following strategy does not attempt to predict the future. It enters the market only after a trend has been confirmed and holds the position until the market changes direction.

Identifying a Trend

The key is determining when a market is genuinely trending. Common tools include moving averages, trend strength indicators, and momentum-based filters.

  • Moving Averages (SMA/EMA): alignment confirms trend direction.
  • ADX (Average Directional Index): values above 25 indicate trend strength.
  • MACD and momentum indicators: confirm trend acceleration.

Entry and Exit Rules

Clear entry and exit rules are essential for systematic execution and long-term consistency.

  • Entry: breakout above a recent N-period high or moving average crossover.
  • Exit / Trailing stop: ATR-based stop-loss combined with trend-following exits.

Validation and Backtesting

Every strategy must be validated through robust backtesting, tested across different market regimes, and evaluated for stability.

Conclusion

A well-designed trend-following system combines objective rules, disciplined risk management, and patience to exploit sustained market trends.
