Creating a Trend Following Strategy

Published on: February 8, 2026

Trend following is one of the most well-established systematic trading methodologies. The core idea is simple: identify an existing trend and follow it until it ends, maximizing profits from directional market movements.

What Is Trend Following

A trend-following strategy does not attempt to predict the future. It enters the market only after a trend has been confirmed and holds the position until the market changes direction.

Identifying a Trend

The key is determining when a market is genuinely trending. Common tools include:

  • Moving Averages (SMA/EMA): for example, the 50 SMA above the 200 SMA confirms an uptrend.
  • ADX (Average Directional Index): values above 25 indicate a strong trend.
  • MACD and momentum indicators: confirm trend acceleration or deceleration.

Entry and Exit Rules

Clear entry and exit rules are essential:

  • Entry: breakout above a recent N-period high or moving average crossover confirmed by momentum.
  • Exit / Trailing stop: ATR-based stop-loss to protect capital, with a trailing stop to follow the trend until reversal.

Risk Management

  • Risk per trade is limited, typically 1â€“2% of total capital.
  • Drawdown control and position sizing adjusted for volatility.
  • Acceptance that most trades may be losing, with a few winning trades compensating for losses.

Validation and Backtesting

Every strategy must be validated through robust backtesting on historical data, tested across different market regimes, and checked for parameter stability over time.

Conclusion

A well-designed trend-following system combines trend indicators, clear entry/exit rules, and rigorous risk management. It is particularly effective during periods of persistent market direction.
